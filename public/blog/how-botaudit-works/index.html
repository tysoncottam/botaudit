How BotAudit works

March 2026 · 5 min read

BotAudit automatically tests your AI support bot — asking real customer questions, multiple times, in a real browser — and grades it A–F on consistency, response quality, and reliability. No API keys. No integrations. Just paste a URL.

We ran it on 8 real companies’ bots. Here’s what we found.

How it works
1

Paste your support page URL

BotAudit auto-detects 15+ chat platforms — Zendesk, Intercom, Ada, Forethought, Drift, and more. No setup required.

2

Choose your questions and run count

Use 20 expert-designed questions across 6 categories, or upload your own. Each question can be asked 1x, 3x, or 5x to measure consistency.

3

BotAudit tests the live bot in a real browser

Every question is sent to your actual chat widget, in a fresh browser session, exactly as a real customer would. Screenshots are captured for every interaction.

4

Get a graded PDF report

Letter grade, executive summary, category breakdown, and specific recommendations — plus a shareable link to view results online.

Semantic Consistency

Goes beyond exact string matching

 ▼

Traditional tools flag AI bots as “inconsistent” just because they rephrase answers. BotAudit uses cosine similarity to classify responses as Identical, Equivalent, Variable, or Contradictory.

Response Quality (1–5)

Did it actually help, or just deflect?

 ▼

Each response is rated on topic relevance, actionable steps, empathy, and whether the bot provided specific guidance or just said “visit our help center.” Ratings: Answered → Partial → Deflected → Escalated → Failed.

Screenshots

See exactly what your customers see

 ▼

Every interaction is captured as a screenshot — the bot’s open state, the question sent, and the response received. Useful for reporting bugs or presenting findings to stakeholders.

Shareable Results Link

Send results to your team without a login

 ▼

Every completed audit gets a permanent shareable URL (e.g. botaudit.app/r/your-id) that anyone can view without an account. Share with your bot vendor, CX team, or leadership.
